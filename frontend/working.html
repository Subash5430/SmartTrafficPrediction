How the Risk Is Decided

Understanding why a location is classified as Low, Medium, or High risk

1. Real-World Conditions

The system evaluates traffic conditions based on where the accident might occur, how many vehicles are present, and the time of day. These factors reflect real-world human activity patterns.

2. Human Movement Patterns

Certain times of the day naturally involve higher movement of people and vehicles. Morning office hours, evening returns, and weekends typically show increased road usage, which raises the likelihood of accidents.

3. Location Sensitivity

Areas with higher population density, commercial zones, junction-heavy roads, and urban centers experience more interactions between vehicles, pedestrians, and road infrastructure, increasing risk.
