AI Model & Data Science Pipeline

Smart Traffic Accident Risk Prediction System

1. Problem Statement

Road accidents arise from complex interactions between traffic density, time, location, and environmental factors. Traditional rule-based systems fail to capture such dynamic behavior. This project presents a deep learning-based accident risk prediction system designed for real-time deployment.

2. Dataset Sources

Two large-scale real-world datasets are used to ensure diversity and robustness.

  • Indian Road Accident Dataset – behavioral, vehicle, and road environment data
  • US Accidents Dataset (Kaggle) – high-resolution spatial, temporal, and weather data

3. Indian Road Accident Dataset

Provides detailed insights into driver behavior, vehicle condition, and accident severity.

  • Temporal: Time, Day_of_week
  • Driver: Age, Gender, Education, Experience
  • Vehicle: Type, Ownership, Service Years, Defects
  • Road & Environment: Junctions, Surface, Weather, Lighting
  • Target Variable: Accident_severity

4. US Accidents Dataset (Kaggle)

Offers fine-grained geospatial, weather, and infrastructure-level information.

  • Geospatial: Start_Lat, Start_Lng
  • Temporal: Start_Time, End_Time
  • Weather: Temperature, Humidity, Visibility, Wind, Precipitation
  • Infrastructure: Traffic_Signal, Junction, Crossing
  • Severity Label

5. Feature Extraction & Harmonization

Common, high-impact features are extracted from both datasets to ensure compatibility and generalization.

  • Latitude & Longitude
  • Hour of Day
  • Day of Week
  • City / Location Context
  • Traffic Volume (derived)
  • Accident Occurrence / Severity

6. Dataset Fusion Strategy

After feature extraction, both datasets are merged into a unified training dataset.

  • Geographic normalization
  • Temporal alignment
  • Traffic scaling
  • Risk label normalization

8. AI Model Architecture

A Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) neural network is used to capture temporal traffic patterns and accident risk evolution.

9. Model Training & Evaluation

  • Supervised learning
  • Loss function: Mean Squared Error (MSE)
  • Optimizer: Adam
  • Time-series windowing

10. Real-Time Prediction Pipeline

User inputs are processed in real time and passed through the trained model. The output is a probability score categorized as Low Risk or High Risk.

11. Key Advantages

  • Cross-region learning using Indian and US datasets
  • Temporal modeling using LSTM
  • Scalable smart city solution
  • Real-time inference capability
